WaterLaneEnd

urn:aero:airm:1.0.0:LogicalModel:Subjects:BaseInfrastructure:AerodromeInfrastructure:WaterLaneEnd

The end of the water lane (typically located at the furthest end of a turning basin) suitable for landing or takeoff runs of aircraft. WaterLaneEnds define the water lane and describe the approach/departure procedure characteristics of a water lane.

Source: FAA (Federal Aviation Administration)
Parent concept: GeoTemporalEnabledEntity

Properties:

Properties Definition Type
airMarkerColour The color of the air marker at the location (if any). CodeColourType
markingBuoy A buoy used to mark the water lane end. MarkingBuoy
width The overall width of the primary water lane. ValDistanceType
isStandardAirMarkerUsed Indicates that a Standard Air Maker is used at the location. The anchor symbol, similar to the designator found on aeronautical charts, is the Standard Air Marker used to designate a seaplane base. The symbol is often painted on roofs or other flat surfaces that are easily visible from the air.

Boolean

name Name given to the water lane end.

CharacterString

depth The depth of the primary water lane with respect to mean lowest low tide. ValVerticalDistanceType
cardinalDirection Code indicating the cardinal compass location of the turning basin from centroid of the WaterLaneEnd. CodeCompassPointType
approachGuidance Identifies the type of approach guidance in use or planned for the Water Lane End.
